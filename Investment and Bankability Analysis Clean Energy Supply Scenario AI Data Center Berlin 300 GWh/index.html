PPA Pricing — Revenue floor, break-even & margin of safety
Revenue in renewable energy is almost entirely a function of the contracted PPA price. LCOE establishes the cost floor — the minimum price at which the project breaks even over its lifetime. The NPV-neutral PPA is the precise price at which NPV equals zero. Every increment above that threshold is margin. The wider the spread between the contracted PPA and the break-even price, the more revenue erosion the project can absorb before debt service is impaired.
PPA vs LCOE vs break-even floor
Blue bars show the LCOE cost floor. Green bars show the contracted PPA. The amber line marks the NPV-neutral break-even. A PPA above both bars and the amber line confirms positive revenue margin.

P90 bankability stress — NPV under yield shortfall
P50 is the base case. P75 applies a 5% energy yield reduction. P90 represents a 1-in-10 year shortfall — the industry standard bankability stress. Positive bars confirm solvency under the lender's required downside scenario.
Stochastic Assessment — 5,000 simulated futures, all variables simultaneously
Rather than shifting one variable at a time, this simulation draws all inputs simultaneously — energy yield, CAPEX, OPEX, PPA price, and cost of equity each sampled from calibrated probability distributions across 5,000 iterations. The result is the full statistical distribution of possible NPV outcomes. P(Positive Return) is the bankability pass mark: below 70% is difficult to defend to a credit committee; above 90% is fundable at investment-grade terms.
NPV distribution — all scenarios
Green bars represent NPV-positive outcomes; red bars represent negative. A narrow distribution signals low uncertainty; a wide spread indicates higher model sensitivity. P10 and P90 are the lender's downside and upside anchors respectively.

Investment Thesis —
A — Portfolio Valuation & IRR Waterfall
NPV above zero confirms value created above the cost of capital. The IRR waterfall decomposes the asset return (Project IRR, unlevered) from the equity return (Equity IRR, levered) — the gap is the financing effect. A positive spread means debt amplifies equity returns above the hurdle rate. The Profitability Index (PI = NPV/CAPEX, Brealey-Myers standard) ranks technologies by value per unit of capital deployed — the primary metric for cross-project capital allocation.

B — Investor Return Profile
Equity IRR above WACC confirms value creation after financing costs. The Equity Alpha (IRR − WACC) is the primary equity IC decision metric. The Profitability Index (PI = NPV/CAPEX) normalises returns against capital deployed, enabling cross-technology capital allocation ranking. MOIC is the gross cash-on-cash multiple to equity holders over the full asset life.

C — Annual Cashflow & Debt Coverage
FCFE is what remains for equity holders after all operating costs, tax, and debt service. CFADS (Cash Flow Available for Debt Service) is the numerator of DSCR — the primary lender covenant metric. A DSCR below the covenant line signals a potential lender trigger event. FCFF (Free Cashflow to Firm) drives the unlevered Project IRR — shown as an overlay option.

D — Debt Service & Lender Metrics
Senior debt is repaid over the loan tenor via annuity payments (constant total payment, declining interest). The DSCR trough year is the lender's primary watch point — breach of the covenant floor triggers loan acceleration provisions. Debt/EBITDA at peak leverage measures gearing quality in the early years, aligned with S&P project finance rating methodology (2014) — ≤6.0x is the generally accepted comfort threshold for renewables infrastructure lending.

F — Stochastic Risk Assessment
Monte Carlo simulates 5,000 joint scenarios — simultaneously varying energy yield (σ=10%), CAPEX (σ=8%), OPEX (σ=7%), PPA price (σ=6%), and cost of equity (σ=0.5pp). G — PPA Pricing Strategy
LCOE is the minimum viable PPA — the floor at which the project breaks even on a lifetime discounted basis (IEA standard: LCOE = PV(CAPEX+OPEX+Fuel) / PV(Energy) at WACC). The Break-even PPA is the exact minimum PPA for NPV=0 (bisection root-find). LCOE Coverage Ratio = PPA/LCOE: ≥1.0x means the contracted price covers cost; every unit above is margin for equity holders.
PPA Price vs LCOE vs NPV-Neutral PPA

H — Bankability & Operational Performance
Senior lender metrics: LLCR ≥ 1.25x and PLCR ≥ 1.20x are IFC/AIIB bankability thresholds. Debt/EBITDA Peak is the maximum gearing ratio within the debt tenor. Capacity Factor and Curtailment Rate are operational bankability inputs. CO₂ Abatement uses IPCC AR6 WG3 (2022) lifecycle emission factors — required for ESG-aligned capital (green bonds, DFI blended finance). Monte Carlo density is approximated from P10/P50/P90 percentiles via a Gaussian fit to the actual simulation distribution.
